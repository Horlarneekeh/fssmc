JUMMAH PRAYER

Jumu’ah prayer is a special congregational prayer held every Friday that replaces the regular Dhuhr prayer. It includes a sermon (khutbah) delivered by the Imam, offering guidance and reminders to strengthen faith and encourage good character.

This weekly gathering brings Muslims together in worship and provides an opportunity for spiritual reflection, community unity, and renewed commitment to living according to Islamic teachings.

    SUNNAH OF JUMU’AH

    • Perform Ghusl (Ritual Bath) It is recommended to perform ghusl before attending the Jumu’ah prayer as part of preparing oneself physically and spiritually for the congregational gathering.
      Wear Clean and Good Clothing Muslims are encouraged to wear clean and presentable clothing when attending Jumu’ah prayer as a sign of respect for the sacred gathering.
      Apply Fragrance (for Men) It is Sunnah for men to apply pleasant fragrance before going to the mosque for Jumu’ah prayer.
      Go Early to the Mosque Arriving early for the Jumu’ah prayer allows worshippers to engage in additional remembrance of Allah and voluntary prayers before the khutbah begins.
      Recite Surah Al-Kahf It is recommended to recite Surah Al-Kahf on Friday. The Prophet ﷺ said that whoever recites it on Friday will have a light that shines for them until the next Friday.
      Send Blessings Upon the Prophet ﷺ Muslims are encouraged to increase salawat (sending blessings) upon the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ on the day of Jumu’ah. Example: Allahumma salli ‘ala Muhammad wa ‘ala ali Muhammad
      Increase Good Deeds Jumu’ah is a day to increase acts of worship such as charity, remembrance of Allah, helping others, and maintaining good character.

    To ensure a peaceful and organized prayer environment, all congregants are encouraged to follow these simple guidelines.

  • Arrive early to allow time for preparation and forming rows
  • Ensure cleanliness and wear modest attire
  • Silence mobile phones before the prayer begins
  • Form straight rows and follow the Imam
  • Maintain silence and focus during the prayer and khutbah
  • Follow instructions from mosque volunteers

    Eid el Fitr

    Eid al-Fitr marks the end of the blessed month of Ramadan, a sacred period during which Muslims around the world dedicate themselves to fasting, prayer, charity, and spiritual reflection. It is a day filled with gratitude, unity, and celebration, as believers thank Allah for granting them the strength, patience, and guidance to complete the month of fasting. After thirty days of self-discipline, increased worship, and acts of kindness, Eid arrives as a joyful occasion that brings families, friends, and entire communities together.

    The Eid prayer is one of the most significant moments of the day. Muslims gather in mosques, prayer grounds, and community centers to perform the special congregational prayer, listen to the sermon, and collectively express gratitude to Allah. This gathering symbolizes the unity of the Muslim community, where people from different backgrounds stand side by side in worship, sharing the same joy and spiritual fulfillment.

    RITES OF EID EL FITR

    • Pay Zakat al-Fitr: Ensure that Zakat al-Fitr is given before the Eid prayer so that those in need can also share in the joy of the day.

      Perform Ghusl: It is recommended to perform ghusl (ritual bath) before leaving for the Eid prayer as part of preparing oneself for this blessed gathering.

      Wear Your Best Clothes: Muslims are encouraged to wear clean and beautiful clothing on Eid. This reflects the joy and honor of the day. Wearing a pleasant fragrance is also recommended for men.

      Eat Before Leaving for the Prayer: It is Sunnah on Eid al-Fitr to eat something before going to the Eid prayer, preferably an odd number of dates.

      Recite the Takbir: Muslims are encouraged to recite the Takbir while heading to the place of Eid prayer until the prayer begins. Allahu Akbar, Allahu Akbar, Allahu Akbar La ilaha illa Allah, Allahu Akbar, Allahu Akbar Wa lillahi al-hamd

      Walk to the Prayer Location (If Possible). If convenient, it is recommended to walk to the Eid prayer location, reflecting humility and devotion.

      Take a Different Route When Returning The Prophet ﷺ used to return from the Eid prayer using a different route from the one taken when going.

      Attend the Eid Prayer Attending the Eid prayer in congregation is one of the most important acts of worship on this day.

      Listen to the Khutbah After the prayer, the Imam delivers the Eid khutbah (sermon). Muslims are encouraged to remain seated and listen attentively.

    • Eid al-Adha

    Eid al-Adha is one of the most significant celebrations in Islam, commemorating the devotion and obedience of Prophet Ibrahim (peace be upon him), who was willing to sacrifice his son in submission to Allah’s command. In His mercy, Allah replaced the sacrifice with a ram, and this act is remembered each year as a powerful symbol of faith, trust, and obedience.

    The day of Eid begins with a special congregational prayer, bringing the community together in gratitude and worship. It is also marked by the act of Qurbani (sacrifice), where those who are able offer an animal, and the meat is shared among family, friends, and those in need, reflecting the values of generosity and compassion.

    Eid al-Adha is a time of unity, charity, and remembrance of Allah. Muslims celebrate by coming together with loved ones, giving to those less fortunate, and reflecting on the importance of sincerity, sacrifice, and devotion in their lives.

    RITES OF EID EL ADHA

    • Perform Ghusl (Ritual Bath) It is recommended to perform ghusl before leaving for the Eid prayer, as an act of physical and spiritual preparation.

      Dress Clean and Modest Wear your best, clean clothing. Men are encouraged to apply fragrance, while all should present themselves in a dignified manner to honor the occasion.

      Do Not Shave or Cut Hair Before Eid It is a Sunnah to avoid shaving, trimming hair, or clipping nails before performing the Eid prayer. This act preserves the sanctity and joy of the day, reflecting the Sunnah of the Prophet ﷺ.

      Do Not Eat Before the Prayer (Eid al-Adha) Unlike Eid al-Fitr, it is recommended not to eat before the Eid prayer. Waiting until after the prayer maintains the traditional etiquette of Eid al-Adha and aligns with the Sunnah.

      Recite the Takbir The Takbir is recited from the 9th Dhu al-Hijjah (Day of Arafah) until the 13th Dhu al-Hijjah: Allahu Akbar, Allahu Akbar, La ilaha illa Allah, Allahu Akbar, Allahu Akbar, wa lillahi al-hamd It can be recited on the way to the mosque, in gatherings, or at home.

      Attend the Eid Prayer Perform the congregational Eid prayer in straight rows, listen attentively to the khutbah, and maintain decorum in the mosque.

      Perform Qurbani (Sacrifice) Carry out the Qurbani after the Eid prayer, dividing the meat into family, friends/relatives, and the needy. Ensure the intention (niyyah) is purely for Allah’s sake.

      Increase in Good Deeds and Charity Eid al-Adha is a time for generosity. Visit family, help those in need, share meals, and engage in acts of kindness and dhikr.
