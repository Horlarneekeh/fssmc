About Islam

Islam is a monotheistic religion based on the belief in one God, Allah, the Creator of the universe. The word Islam means submission to the will of Allah, and Muslims believe that following His guidance leads to a balanced and meaningful life.

Muslims believe that Allah sent many prophets throughout history, including Adam, Noah, Abraham, Moses, and Jesus (peace be upon them all), with Prophet Muhammad ﷺ being the final messenger who delivered the Qur’an, the holy book of Islam.

Today, Islam is practiced by millions of people around the world, united by their faith in Allah, the Qur’an, and the teachings of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ.

Pillars of Islam

The Five Pillars of Islam are the fundamental acts of worship that form the foundation of a Muslim’s faith and practice. They provide a framework for spiritual growth, discipline, and devotion to Allah.

01

Shahadah (Faith)

"There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah." The declaration that affirms the core belief of Islam.

02

Salah (Prayer)

Five daily prayers performed at specific times. They serve as regular moments of remembrance and connection with Allah.

03

Zakat (Charity)

The obligation for Muslims who are financially able to give a portion of their wealth to those in need, purifying one's wealth.

04

Sawm (Fasting)

Fasting during the month of Ramadan from dawn until sunset, encouraging self-discipline, patience, and gratitude.

05

Hajj (Pilgrimage)

The pilgrimage to Makkah that every Muslim who is physically and financially able should perform at least once.
