Jumu’ah Prayer

Jumu’ah prayer is a special congregational prayer held every Friday that replaces the regular Dhuhr prayer. It includes a sermon (khutbah) delivered by the Imam, offering guidance and reminders to strengthen faith and encourage good character.

This weekly gathering brings Muslims together in worship and provides an opportunity for spiritual reflection, community unity, and renewed commitment to living according to Islamic teachings.

Sunnah of Jumu’ah

  • Perform Ghusl Recommended ritual bath before attending to prepare physically and spiritually.
  • Wear Clean Clothing Dress presentably as a sign of respect for the sacred gathering.
  • Apply Fragrance It is Sunnah for men to apply pleasant fragrance before going to the mosque.
  • Go Early Arrive early to engage in remembrance and voluntary prayers.
  • Recite Surah Al-Kahf Provides a light that shines until the next Friday.
  • Send Blessings upon the Prophet ﷺ Increase salawat on the day of Jumu’ah.

Taraweeh Schedule

Join us for the blessed nightly Taraweeh prayers during the month of Ramadan. These special prayers are performed after ‘Isha and provide a beautiful opportunity to stand in worship, listen to the recitation of the Qur’an, and strengthen our connection with Allah.

Taraweeh will be led by our Imam, with portions of the Qur’an recited each night throughout Ramadan. We welcome the entire community to join us in these blessed nights.

Eid Celebrations

Eid al-Fitr

End of Ramadan

Marks the end of the blessed month of Ramadan. It is a day filled with gratitude, unity, and celebration, as believers thank Allah for granting them the strength to complete the month of fasting.

The Eid prayer is one of the most significant moments. Muslims gather to perform the special congregational prayer, listen to the sermon, and collectively express gratitude to Allah.

Rites of Eid al-Fitr

  • Pay Zakat al-Fitr: Ensure it is given before the prayer.
  • Perform Ghusl: Ritual bath before leaving.
  • Eat Before Leaving: Sunnah to eat an odd number of dates before the prayer.
  • Recite Takbir: Allahu Akbar, Allahu Akbar... on the way to the mosque.
  • Change Routes: Take a different route when returning home.

Eid al-Adha

Festival of Sacrifice

Commemorates the devotion of Prophet Ibrahim (peace be upon him). The day begins with a special congregational prayer followed by the act of Qurbani (sacrifice).

It is a time of unity, charity, and remembrance of Allah where the meat is shared among family, friends, and those in need, reflecting the values of generosity and compassion.

Rites of Eid al-Adha

  • No Hair/Nail Clipping: Avoid cutting hair/nails in the days before until after the prayer.
  • Perform Ghusl: Ritual bath and wear clean clothes.
  • Do Not Eat Before Prayer: Wait until after the prayer to eat (preferably from the sacrifice).
  • Recite Takbir: From 9th to 13th Dhu al-Hijjah.
  • Perform Qurbani: After the prayer, divide meat among family, friends, and the needy.
