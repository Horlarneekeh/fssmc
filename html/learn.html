ABOUT ISLAM

Islam is a monotheistic religion based on the belief in one God, Allah, the Creator of the universe. The word Islam means submission to the will of Allah, and Muslims believe that following His guidance leads to a balanced and meaningful life.Muslims believe that Allah sent many prophets throughout history, including Adam, Noah, Abraham, Moses, and Jesus (peace be upon them all), with Prophet Muhammad ﷺ being the final messenger who delivered the Qur’an, the holy book of Islam.The foundation of Islamic practice is the Five Pillars of Islam: the declaration of faith (Shahadah), daily prayers (Salah), charity (Zakat), fasting in Ramadan (Sawm), and pilgrimage to Makkah (Hajj). Islam also teaches values such as kindness, justice, honesty, and compassion. oday, Islam is practiced by millions of people around the world, united by their faith in Allah, the Qur’an, and the teachings of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ.

PILLARS OF ISLAM

The Five Pillars of Islam are the fundamental acts of worship that form the foundation of a Muslim’s faith and practice. They provide a framework for spiritual growth, discipline, and devotion to Allah. Through these pillars, Muslims strengthen their relationship with their Creator and cultivate values such as gratitude, compassion, and humility.

SHAHADA

The Shahadah is the declaration that affirms the core belief of Islam: “There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah.” This testimony expresses a Muslim’s belief in the oneness of Allah and acceptance of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ as His final messenger. It is the first and most essential pillar of Islam.

SALAH(PRAYER)

Salah refers to the five daily prayers performed by Muslims at specific times throughout the day. These prayers serve as regular moments of remembrance, allowing believers to pause from daily activities and reconnect with Allah. Prayer strengthens faith, promotes discipline, and reminds Muslims of their dependence on Allah in all aspects of life.

ZAKAT(ALMSGIVING)

Zakat is the obligation for Muslims who are financially able to give a portion of their wealth to those in need. It is a means of purifying one’s wealth and helping support the welfare of the community. Through Zakat, Islam encourages generosity, compassion, and social responsibility.

SAWM(FASTING)

During the month of Ramadan, Muslims fast from dawn until sunset by abstaining from food, drink, and other physical needs. Fasting is an act of devotion that encourages self-discipline, patience, and gratitude. It also helps believers develop empathy for those who are less fortunate while increasing their spiritual awareness.

HALL(PILGRIMAGE)

Hajj is the pilgrimage to the holy city of Makkah that every Muslim who is physically and financially able should perform at least once in their lifetime. The pilgrimage brings together Muslims from around the world in a powerful demonstration of unity, humility, and devotion to Allah.
